About Me

Adventure + Venture + Technology

20% soft skills / 80% hard skills / ∞ motivation

Yes, that's the proportion of my capabilities in each of these domains. At least, that's what I think it is.

I started my career in a pretty conventional way - an Electrical engineer from National Institute of Technology Karnataka, Surathkal. But I guess, that's the most unimportant part of me - what's more interesting is the series of events that followed.


Fast forwarding that to 2021, I have had the opportunities to work at Goldman Sachs, Grab and Visa which spawned across timezones from -0800H to +0800H. Additionally, I have had my share of successes in cracking interviews at several companies like Facebook and Apple, have ventured into startups (which didn't go well), travelled across the world, met people with various ethinicities and motivated tons of people to try their luck at interviewing the "dream" companies.

Okay stop bragging pls, HOW???


Fail. Learn. Repeat.

Embracing failures is what drives me to success. I truly believe 99.999% of the skills which we aspire of having are aquired ones, and these are aquired by failing at things which many others succeed. But given enough effort, time and will power, there is nothing that can stop us from aquiring any skill. And for me, this is the mantra to follow and lead a life towards success.

If earning money is the only meter of success for you, then I am definitely not the person who could motivate you to "succeed".

I started the journey with absolutely no knowledge of "software engineering". If you have ever laughed at a person calling "#stdio.h" as "studio", I was that guy in university. But building software was a domain that always excited me since I could build and ship products while sitting in my hostel room and with a laptop and 8mbps internet connection.
It's wrong to say I am a self taught engineer - I was taught by Youtube, Coursera, Wikipedia, Geeksforgeeks and even my friends' college projects. The only thing which I had in me for all this while was a zeal to try and learn new things.

What I Do


  • I write code
  • I motivate people to write code
  • I identify products which users want
  • I solve developer painpoints
  • I write code

I think it's safe to say that I spend majority of my time in building applications that scale. I work on solving some exciting problems in the Margin Engineering domain at Goldman Sachs. Together with the team, we go forward with one mission - to make sure that the ghosts of 2008 financial crisis never step back on the porches of the banking industry (and Goldman Sachs).

As an engineer, I primarily focus towards building an in memory graph for all the positions and their associated risks of Goldman Sachs' clients, and gluing infrascture components in a way that it's maintainable, scalable and fault tolerant.

I like spending my leisure time in helping other engineers how they can prepare for interviews, aquire targeted skills which the industry needs and build softwares that can drive revolutionary changes.

“If there is one thing that I absolutely suck at, it is the ability of appreciating people whom I don't find inspiring.”

It's just an ability which I have purposely never developed - to say things which I personally don't believe in. And don't get me wrong - it's very easy to make me an admirer of a quality in you.


I like building products for the community as well. I have been appreciated a lot for my ability to critically analyze products, and point out user meaningful user behaviours. As a result, I have recently started venturing into building certain developer-focused and customer focused products.

Most of these projects spawn across a few months and create a baseline for others to follow - but I am always ears for any exciting idea that you have in mind and need my input/help/contribution.

If you think your ideas could excite me into contributing, contact me !
